Structs are one of the two ways of creating [[Objects]] in C++, the other being [[Classes]]. They are functionally identical with the only difference being that the data members and functions of structs default to public while classes default to private meaning that by default third party code (such as main) cannot access said functions and data members. As they are identical to classes most of the implementation of them will be contained within the [[Objects]] note to save space.
